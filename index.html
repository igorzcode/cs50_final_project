What you're seeing

The setup

Each agent emits one of 10 possible sounds and holds beliefs about what each sound means. Initially these are random — pure noise. No sound carries meaning.

The feedback loop

When an agent finds food, it calls out. Nearby agents hear, walk toward it, and if they find food too, they reinforce their belief: that sound means food. Useful sounds get reinforced. Useless ones fade.

The emergence

Over time, one sound statistically wins. The population converges on a shared signal — a word — without any agent knowing it's building a language. Meaning emerges from reinforcement, not design.

The mutations

Three forces resist consensus: isolated agents drift to new sounds, young agents experiment, and signals get slightly mis-copied. These mirror real linguistic processes — dialect, slang, and phonetic shift.
